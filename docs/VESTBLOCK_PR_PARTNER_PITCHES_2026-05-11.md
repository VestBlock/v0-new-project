# VestBlock PR And Partner Pitch Angles

Last updated: 2026-05-11

## Positioning Guardrails

Lead with:

- cleaner records
- proof and approval history
- funding readiness before applications
- visibility systems instead of vague marketing retainers
- AI receptionist and booking support for service businesses

Avoid:

- fake traction claims
- fake partner or customer logos
- funding guarantees
- legal-enforceability promises
- escrow replacement framing
- tokenization and investment language

## Current Proof Points From The Product

- DealVault has a live public product page, demo path, dashboard routes, pilot form, certificate generation, and proof-layer smart contract support.
- DealVault messaging already states that private documents stay off-chain and blockchain is used for proof, timestamps, and event records.
- The funding funnel offers a free eligibility check plus a paid prep path before partner applications.
- Visibility Expansion has three concrete packages: `Visibility Starter`, `City Expansion Engine`, and `Authority PR Engine`.
- AI receptionist has three concrete offers: `AI Receptionist Launch`, `AI Receptionist + Appointment Booking`, and `Website Upgrade Sprint`.

## Copy Starters

### Local business media

Subject idea: Small businesses are paying for clearer proof trails, not just more apps

One-line hook:
VestBlock is seeing a practical demand from local operators who want one cleaner record for agreements, proof, approvals, and payout status without exposing sensitive documents on-chain.

### Fintech newsletters

Subject idea: Why funding prep deserves its own product before lender applications

One-line hook:
VestBlock is packaging the work that usually gets skipped before small businesses apply for funding: readiness checks, document cleanup, business setup gaps, and clearer next-step guidance.

### Real estate operator groups

Subject idea: A cleaner way to track partner splits, proof, and payout status

One-line hook:
DealVault gives smaller real estate operators a simpler record of what was agreed, what proof was submitted, who approved it, and what is ready to pay.

### Contractor and service business groups

Subject idea: Stop losing milestone proof in crew phones and text threads

One-line hook:
VestBlock can pitch DealVault as a job-tracking record for proof, approvals, and payout readiness when the office and field team keep drifting apart.

### Small business podcasts

Subject idea: Two systems small businesses need now: answer-engine visibility and after-hours lead capture

One-line hook:
VestBlock has a practical operator story about why small businesses need better search visibility and AI receptionist coverage before they buy another vague marketing retainer.

### Funding communities

Subject idea: More owners need a funding-readiness review than another lender link

One-line hook:
VestBlock helps businesses figure out whether to pursue partner options now or fix documents, business setup, and credit issues first.

### Partner newsletters

Subject idea: A useful partner insert for businesses choosing their next growth bottleneck

One-line hook:
VestBlock can supply short educational blurbs around DealVault records, funding prep, visibility systems, or AI receptionist setup without making outcome guarantees.

## Recommended Research Queue

1. Build one spreadsheet tab per audience above.
2. Capture: publication or group name, audience type, geography, contact path, recent topics, and which VestBlock angle fits.
3. Prioritize targets that already publish:
   - operator case studies
   - local service-business tactics
   - funding-readiness advice
   - proptech workflow stories
   - contractor operations content
4. Skip outlets that mainly want venture-scale funding stories, crypto framing, or legal-tech claims.

## Best Immediate Move

Start with three parallel mini-campaigns instead of one broad blast:

1. DealVault for real estate and contractor operators.
2. Funding readiness for fintech newsletters and funding communities.
3. Visibility plus AI receptionist for podcasts and partner newsletters.

That split matches the real product surface and keeps the proof points clear.
