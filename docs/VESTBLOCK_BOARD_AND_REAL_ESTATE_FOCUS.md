# VestBlock Board And Real Estate Focus

## Recommended Board

VestBlock should use a small advisory board built around real estate deal movement, not a generic software board.

1. Real estate operator chair: Owns the core customer reality across acquisitions, dispositions, seller conversations, buyer fit, assignment structure, and team workflows.
2. Private lending and capital advisor: Reviews DSCR, hard money, rental, fix-and-flip, private lending, borrower fit, and lender routing strategy.
3. Title, escrow, and compliance advisor: Keeps DealVault positioning clear around proof records without implying legal, escrow, brokerage, custody, title, or financing guarantees.
4. Buyer network and disposition advisor: Shapes buy boxes, partner criteria, proof-of-funds expectations, buyer segmentation, and opportunity routing.
5. PropTech product advisor: Helps turn seller intake, buyer/lender criteria, DealVault records, and partner portals into a coherent operating product.
6. Growth and local authority advisor: Keeps Search Visibility, AI Receptionist, and website systems positioned as support around real estate operators instead of separate competing businesses.

## Strategic Angle

VestBlock should focus on becoming a real estate deal operating layer: source, review, fund, route, and prove real estate deals.

DealVault stays the flagship product, but the front door should be real estate: seller intake, buyer/lender routing, funding review, and proof records. Search Visibility, AI Receptionist, business funding prep, and credit support remain available, but they should support the real estate deal path instead of sharing equal homepage weight.

## Main Plans To Feature

1. Seller Property Review: Property owners submit details for cash, creative, investor, or referral review.
2. Real Estate Funding And Buyer/Lender Routing: Investors, buyers, lenders, and partners submit DSCR, rental, flip, hard-money, buy-box, or funding-fit details.
3. DealVault Proof Layer: Real estate teams track agreement records, payout splits, proof certificates, and milestone history.
4. Paid Real Estate Deal Review: Higher-touch review for deals that need lender-facing or partner-facing preparation.
5. Support Services: Search Visibility, AI Receptionist, business funding prep, setup, grants, and credit tools stay behind the primary real estate paths.

## Board Decision Rules

Use the board to make hard calls. If an idea does not help VestBlock source, review, fund, route, or prove real estate deals, it should be delayed, reframed, or removed from the main website.

Decision filter:
1. Real estate operator chair: Does this help a real operator move a seller, buyer, lender, or partner conversation forward?
2. Private lending and capital advisor: Does this improve deal finance clarity, borrower fit, lender fit, or underwriting readiness without implying approval?
3. Title, escrow, and compliance advisor: Does this avoid claims that sound like legal, brokerage, title, escrow, custody, investment, or financing guarantees?
4. Buyer network and disposition advisor: Does this make buy boxes, proof of funds, buyer appetite, partner criteria, or opportunity routing more useful?
5. PropTech product advisor: Does this belong inside the product workflow, or is it just marketing copy?
6. Growth and local authority advisor: Does this bring better qualified real estate demand, or is it generic traffic?

Board vetoes:
- Do not make credit repair, grants, broad business funding, AI receptionist, or SEO the homepage lead.
- Do not restart automated email outreach until daily lead quality, sender reputation, opt-out handling, and manual approval are working.
- Do not describe DealVault as legal escrow, title, custody, brokerage, guaranteed proof of ownership, or guaranteed payment enforcement.
- Do not add new service pages unless they support seller intake, buyer/lender routing, DealVault, or real estate deal review.

## Current Board Decisions

1. VestBlock's main business is real estate deal infrastructure.
2. The homepage should sell the real estate path first: seller review, buyer/lender routing, DealVault.
3. DealVault should be positioned as the premium proof and accountability layer for real estate deal teams.
4. The buyer and lender network should become a structured data asset: buy boxes, markets, deal types, proof-of-funds status, lending criteria, response history, and relationship stage.
5. Offline outreach should focus on relationship-building with buyers, lenders, title/escrow contacts, agents, wholesalers, and local investor groups, but messages should stay drafted or manually approved until deliverability is stable.
6. Search Visibility and AI Receptionist should be packaged as add-ons for real estate operators, not as equal company pillars.
7. Pricing should push clear first actions before complex packages: submit property, submit funding deal, request DealVault demo, request paid deal review.
8. The next product priority should be partner criteria capture: buyer portal, lender portal, buy-box intake, lender-fit intake, and admin scoring.

## Board Optimization Prompt

Use this prompt when reviewing VestBlock strategy, copy, features, pricing, outreach, or automations:

```text
You are the VestBlock board of directors. Act as six advisors:

1. Real Estate Operator Chair
2. Private Lending And Capital Advisor
3. Title, Escrow, And Compliance Advisor
4. Buyer Network And Disposition Advisor
5. PropTech Product Advisor
6. Growth And Local Authority Advisor

Your job is to make practical decisions for VestBlock, not brainstorm broadly.

Current company focus:
VestBlock is a real estate deal operating layer that helps source, review, fund, route, and prove real estate deals. The main website and product hierarchy should prioritize Seller Property Review, Real Estate Funding And Buyer/Lender Routing, DealVault Proof Layer, and Paid Real Estate Deal Review. Search Visibility, AI Receptionist, business funding prep, grants, setup, and credit tools are support services only.

Review the proposed idea, page, feature, outreach campaign, automation, or pricing change using these questions:
- Does it help a seller, buyer, lender, investor, partner, or operator move a real estate deal forward?
- Does it strengthen DealVault, seller intake, buyer/lender routing, or real estate deal review?
- Does it avoid legal, escrow, brokerage, custody, investment, credit, funding, ranking, traffic, or revenue guarantees?
- Does it improve lead quality, partner criteria, proof records, follow-up clarity, or conversion?
- Is it important enough for the homepage or should it live as a support service?
- What should be deleted, delayed, simplified, or moved lower?

Output:
1. Board vote: approve, revise, delay, or reject.
2. Final decision in one sentence.
3. Advisor comments, one short paragraph per advisor.
4. Required changes ranked by priority.
5. Website/product/outreach impact.
6. Compliance or deliverability concerns.
7. The smallest next action that should be implemented now.

Be direct. If a feature dilutes the real estate focus, say so and recommend where it should move or whether it should be removed.
```
