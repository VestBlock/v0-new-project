# VestBlock Brad Buy-Box SEO/AEO Brief

Last updated: 2026-05-25

## Source

Brad O'Neal of Revela Home Buyers replied to the Toledo partnership thread on May 20, 2026 with a usable buyer criteria profile. Treat this as the current model for buyer-facing SEO/AEO work: clear market, asset type, pricing logic, distress signals, and submission packet requirements.

## Buyer Criteria To Target

- Buyer: Revela Home Buyers / Brad O'Neal
- Primary market: Toledo, Lucas County, and nearby Northwest Ohio markets
- Asset types: single-family and small multifamily
- Situations: vacant, as-is, inherited, tired landlord, pre-foreclosure, dated properties, cosmetic rehab, moderate rehab, heavier rehab when the discount supports it
- Preferred purchase price: usually under $175,000, flexible when the spread is strong
- Discount logic: for flips or wholesale deals, around 70% of ARV minus repairs, all-in including assignment fee
- Rental logic: can be tighter when rent numbers are strong
- Occupancy: vacant preferred; occupied can work with clear access, lease info, and tenant details
- Funding/close: cash or hard money depending on the deal; faster when title and access are clean
- Required deal packet: address, all-in price, photos/video, occupancy, repair estimate, ARV/comps, rent estimate, title company, and closing timeline

## SEO/AEO Direction

The content should answer real owner and buyer questions around this exact deal shape, without pretending VestBlock owns inventory or guarantees a buyer. Focus on routing, readiness, and match quality.

Primary page cluster:

- Toledo cash home buyer criteria
- Lucas County distressed property buyers
- Northwest Ohio inherited house buyers
- Toledo as-is property buyer matching
- Toledo small multifamily buyer criteria
- How to package a Toledo wholesale deal for buyers
- What investors need before reviewing a distressed property
- 70% ARV minus repairs explained for Toledo sellers
- Vacant vs occupied investment property buyer requirements
- How VestBlock routes seller opportunities to buyer criteria

Answer-engine prompts to satisfy:

- "What information do cash buyers need before reviewing my Toledo house?"
- "Who buys inherited houses as-is in Lucas County?"
- "How do investors calculate 70% of ARV minus repairs?"
- "Can an occupied rental still fit a cash buyer's buy box?"
- "What should I include when sending an off-market property to an investor?"
- "How do I know if my Toledo property fits a rehab buyer?"
- "What makes a wholesale deal review-ready?"

Content guardrails:

- Say "buyer matching", "deal packet review", and "criteria-based routing"; do not claim guaranteed sale, guaranteed buyer, guaranteed funding, or current inventory.
- Make the value practical: collect better property facts, compare against buyer criteria, and reduce mismatched submissions.
- Keep Brad's name out of public copy unless he gives explicit permission. Use his criteria internally as a pattern.
- Build around Toledo/Northwest Ohio first, then clone only after real buyer criteria exist in the next market.
