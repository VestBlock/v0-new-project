# DealVault Prospect Segments

Last updated: 2026-05-11

## What Changed Since 2026-05-09

This pass keeps the existing May 8 and May 9 operator buckets, but prioritizes narrower prospect slices that are:

- smaller-business friendly
- agreement-heavy without requiring enterprise procurement
- likely to have recurring payout, approval, proof, or deliverable friction
- easy to source through safe directories, public rosters, and manual CSV imports

## Positioning Guardrails

Lead with:

- agreement tracking
- milestone and approval history
- payout visibility
- referral accountability
- proof of delivered work
- one shared record

Avoid leading with:

- blockchain-first language
- tokenization
- escrow replacement claims
- legal-enforceability promises
- investment or yield framing

Safe plain-English positioning:

DealVault helps small operator teams keep one clean record of what was agreed, what was delivered, who approved it, and what is ready to pay so fewer deals get stuck in inboxes, texts, and spreadsheets.

## Priority 1 Segments

### 1. Specialty-trade subcontractors on staged draws

- Why now: tighter fit than generic construction and easier to search by trade category
- Search terms:
  - `roofing subcontractor`
  - `electrical subcontractor`
  - `hvac subcontractor`
  - `concrete subcontractor`
  - `framing contractor progress billing`
  - `subcontractor draw schedule`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper trade categories
  - Yelp contractor categories through the existing actor path
  - local builder and subcontractor association directories where terms allow
  - manual CSV imports from regional trade rosters
- Outreach angle: keep photos, milestone approvals, and payout readiness tied to each phase of the job
- Buyer pain: partial-completion arguments, change-order drift, draw delays, proof buried on crew phones
- Plain-English positioning: track what part of the job was finished, who approved it, and what can be paid now

### 2. Restoration, mitigation, and emergency cleanup operators

- Why now: urgent jobs create faster proof and approval pain than slower general-contracting workflows
- Search terms:
  - `water damage restoration company`
  - `fire damage restoration`
  - `mold remediation company`
  - `emergency mitigation services`
  - `contents restoration company`
  - `restoration project manager`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper
  - restoration-industry member directories where usage allows
  - insurance-vendor rosters for manual research
  - local chamber and BNI-style directories
- Outreach angle: keep before/after proof, job-status approvals, and vendor handoffs visible during fast-moving jobs
- Buyer pain: proof scattered across phones, insurer or owner approvals delayed, crews and office running off different updates
- Plain-English positioning: keep each job’s proof, approval, and payout status easier to verify

### 3. Small private-lending broker shops and referral desks

- Why now: better fit than larger lenders because fee chains and intro ownership are messier
- Search terms:
  - `private lending broker`
  - `hard money broker`
  - `commercial loan broker small business`
  - `business funding referral partner`
  - `loan packaging service`
  - `capital advisory boutique`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper
  - broker / ISO directories where permitted
  - lender partner pages for manual list building
  - partner roster CSV imports
- Outreach angle: lock down intro source, fee expectations, and payout status from first referral through funding close
- Buyer pain: overlapping introductions, fee disputes, no timestamped referral trail, spreadsheet payout logs drift over time
- Plain-English positioning: keep referral deals and fee status visible from first intro through payout

### 4. Boutique property managers with maintenance-heavy portfolios

- Why now: they coordinate many small approvals and vendor payouts without enterprise tooling
- Search terms:
  - `boutique property management`
  - `rental turnover services`
  - `small apartment management company`
  - `vacation rental property manager`
  - `hoa property management small portfolio`
  - `maintenance coordination property management`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper
  - NARPM-style member lists where permitted

  - manual market-by-market manager lists
- Outreach angle: connect owner approvals, vendor proof, and payment-ready status across work orders and turns
- Buyer pain: work completed without proof, owner questions, missed turn items, messy vendor handoffs
- Plain-English positioning: one place to confirm what was approved, what got done, and what is ready to pay

## Recommended First Build Order

1. Specialty-trade subcontractors on staged draws
2. Small private-lending broker shops and referral desks
3. Boutique property managers with maintenance-heavy portfolios
4. Restoration, mitigation, and emergency cleanup operators
5. Small recruiting and staffing firms with contingent fees

## Why These Five First

- They are easy to find through current local-business discovery paths.
- Their pain is operational and recurring rather than legal-tech or enterprise-procurement heavy.
- They map directly to agreement records, milestone proof, approval history, vendor accountability, and payout timing.
- They can be pitched safely in plain English without leading with blockchain language.

## Disqualifiers for Early Outreach Lists

Skip or deprioritize:

- enterprise general contractors with heavy custom ERP stacks
- large PE-backed staffing brands
- institutional property managers with rigid corporate procurement
- lenders or brokers leading with securities or investment syndication language
- legal matters that sound like escrow, custody, trust-account, or title-company replacement
- prospects whose problem is mainly CRM, bookkeeping, or e-signature rather than approval/proof/payout visibility
