# DealVault Prospect Segments

Last updated: 2026-05-09

## What Changed Since 2026-05-08

This pass pushes beyond the broad May 8 segment list and narrows in on smaller, more concrete operator niches that:

- manage recurring approvals, milestones, or deliverables
- face payout disputes or "who said what" confusion
- can be found through safe business directories, member lists, or manual CSV imports
- can be positioned with plain operational language instead of blockchain language

## Positioning Guardrails

Lead with:

- agreement tracking
- approval history
- payout visibility
- milestone proof
- deliverable status
- one shared record

Avoid leading with:

- blockchain
- on-chain
- escrow substitute
- legal guarantees
- investment language
- tokenization

Safe plain-English positioning:

DealVault helps small businesses keep a clean record of what was agreed, what was delivered, who approved it, and what is ready to pay so fewer deals get stuck in inboxes, texts, and spreadsheets.

## Priority 1 Segments

### 1. Specialty trade subcontractors with staged payouts

- Why this is new: narrower and easier to source than general construction; strong milestone and proof pain
- Search terms:
  - `roofing contractor`
  - `electrical contractor`
  - `hvac contractor`
  - `flooring installer`
  - `concrete contractor`
  - `subcontractor progress billing`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper category searches
  - Yelp contractor categories through existing actor path
  - local builder association directories where allowed
  - manual CSVs from regional subcontractor rosters
- Outreach angle: keep staged work, photos, approvals, and payout readiness in one place
- Buyer pain: partial completion arguments, field-photo proof gaps, GC approval delays, draw confusion
- Plain-English positioning: track what part of the job was finished, who approved it, and what can be paid now

### 2. Restoration and mitigation companies

- Why this is new: vendor proof and fast approvals matter more than broad contractor positioning
- Search terms:
  - `water damage restoration`
  - `fire damage restoration`
  - `mold remediation company`
  - `emergency mitigation services`
  - `property restoration contractor`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper
  - IICRC-style member directories where usage allows
  - insurance-vendor and chamber directories for manual research
- Outreach angle: organize job approvals, completion proof, and vendor handoff records when jobs move quickly
- Buyer pain: before/after proof scattered across phones, insurance/owner approvals delayed, status confusion across crews
- Plain-English positioning: keep every restoration job’s proof, approval, and payment status easier to verify

## Recommended First Build Order

1. Specialty trade subcontractors with staged payouts
2. Private-lending broker shops and funding referral teams
3. Boutique property managers with heavy maintenance coordination
4. Restoration and mitigation companies
5. Boutique staffing and recruiting firms with contingent fees

## Why These Five First

- They are smaller-business friendly and easier to find in local-business discovery sources.
- Their pain is operational and document-heavy, not abstract legal-tech pain.
- They map cleanly to agreements, payouts, approvals, proofs, and milestone status.
- They can be positioned safely without promising legal enforcement or leading with blockchain.

## Disqualifiers for Early Outreach Lists

Skip or deprioritize:

- one-person consultants with no recurring approvals or payouts
- firms selling mostly one-time low-ticket work
- businesses without partner, vendor, contractor, or milestone coordination
- enterprise accounts that likely need procurement-heavy workflows first
- niches where DealVault would sound like legal software instead of operational recordkeeping
