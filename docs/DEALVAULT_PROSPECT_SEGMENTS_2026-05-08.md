# DealVault Prospect Segments

Last updated: 2026-05-08

## Positioning Guardrails

Lead with:

- agreement tracking
- payout visibility
- milestone proof
- approval records
- audit trail

Avoid leading with:

- blockchain jargon
- tokenization
- escrow claims
- legal-enforceability guarantees
- investment-return language

Safe plain-English positioning:

DealVault helps small operators keep clean records around agreements, referrals, partner splits, milestones, and delivered work so fewer payouts, approvals, and deadlines get lost in text threads and scattered files.

## Priority 1 Segments

### 1. Construction and rehab operators

- Why first: fast pain, repeat milestones, proof-heavy work, high disagreement risk
- Search terms:
  - `small rehab contractor`
  - `general contractor remodeler`
  - `construction draw schedule`
  - `rehab project management company`
  - `home renovation contractor`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper niches already supported
  - Yelp contractor/remodeler categories through existing actor path
  - local builder association member directories where terms allow
  - manual CSV imports from vetted local contractor lists
- Outreach angle: track milestone completion, draw approvals, and proof before payout disputes start
- Buyer pain: change orders buried in email, unclear completion proof, draw disputes, subcontractor timing gaps
- Plain-English positioning: keep a shared record of what was agreed, what was finished, and what is ready to pay

### 2. Property managers with vendor networks

- Why first: recurring work orders, vendor approvals, owner reporting, distributed contractors
- Search terms:
  - `property management company`
  - `rental property manager`
  - `maintenance coordination property management`
  - `turnkey property management`
  - `small apartment management company`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper
  - NARPM-style member directories where permitted
  - local apartment association directories where permitted
  - manual imports from operator-maintained market lists
- Outreach angle: create proof around work orders, vendor completion, and owner-facing accountability
- Buyer pain: vendors say work is done, owners want proof, turnover work slips, approvals live in text chains
- Plain-English positioning: one clean record for who approved the work, what got done, and what is ready for payment

### 3. Referral-driven real estate teams and partner networks

- Why first: frequent split confusion, handshake deals, partner payout sensitivity
- Search terms:
  - `real estate investor`
  - `wholesale real estate`
  - `co wholesale`
  - `acquisitions dispositions real estate`
  - `real estate referral network`
- Safe source ideas:
  - existing real-estate lead imports
  - local investor meetups and association pages for manual list building
  - Google Places for investor, property buyer, and wholesaler terms
  - manual partner-network CRM imports
- Outreach angle: document referral terms, partner splits, and payout status before memory gaps turn into disputes
- Buyer pain: unclear split math, intro-source disputes, missing timestamps, no shared payout ledger
- Plain-English positioning: prove when the deal terms were logged and keep partner payouts visible from intro to close

### 4. Private lenders and hard-money operators

- Why first: milestone-dependent draws, borrower updates, proof requests, recurring document handoffs

  - `bridge lender small balance`

  - Google Places / Outscraper

- Outreach angle: track borrower milestones, draw proofs, and agreement records without adding another messy spreadsheet
- Buyer pain: borrowers send incomplete proof, draw reviews are fragmented, servicing notes are inconsistent
- Plain-English positioning: keep each loan’s milestones, approvals, and proof records in one place

## Recommended First-Round Prioritization

1. Construction and rehab operators
2. Property managers with vendor networks
3. Referral-driven real estate teams and partner networks
4. Private lenders and hard-money operators
5. Funding brokers and referral-based finance shops

These five niches have the clearest overlap with current DealVault messaging, the strongest agreement-and-proof pain, and the safest path through existing local-business discovery channels.
