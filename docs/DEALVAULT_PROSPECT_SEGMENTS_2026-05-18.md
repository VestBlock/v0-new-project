# DealVault Prospect Segments

Last updated: 2026-05-18

## What Changed Since 2026-05-11

This pass keeps the May 11 priority stack, but moves the next target wave toward narrower small-business slices that are:

- easier to source through public directories and manual list building
- more likely to feel milestone, deliverable, or payout friction weekly
- less likely to require enterprise procurement or deep legal review to pilot
- strong fits for plain-English proof-record positioning before blockchain language

## Positioning Guardrails

Lead with:

- one shared record
- milestone and approval history
- referral and split accountability
- proof of delivered work
- payout readiness
- cleaner handoffs between field, office, partner, and client

Avoid leading with:

- crypto language
- tokenization
- escrow replacement claims
- legal enforceability claims
- guaranteed collections or dispute outcomes

Safe plain-English positioning:

DealVault helps smaller operator teams keep one clean record of what was agreed, what got done, who approved it, and what is ready to pay so the details do not stay trapped in inboxes, screenshots, and spreadsheets.

## Priority 1 Segments

### 1. Public adjusters and claims-side coordination shops

- Why now: small firms sit between owner, carrier, and restoration vendor updates, so proof and fee friction show up fast
- Search terms:
  - `public adjuster`
  - `insurance claim consultant`
  - `loss consultant`
  - `proof of loss help`
  - `contents inventory service`
  - `claim documentation company`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper
  - state public-adjuster license directories for manual review
  - restoration partner rosters
  - local chamber and insurance-service directories
- Outreach angle: keep claim milestones, supporting proof, and fee-status handoffs visible from intake through settlement
- Buyer pain: photos and documents are scattered, carrier requests get buried, vendor updates drift, fee status becomes a memory problem
- Plain-English positioning: keep claim proof, status updates, and payout checkpoints easier to confirm

### 2. Low-voltage, security, access-control, and AV integrators

- Why now: these teams often bill against staged installs, approvals, and completion proof without large back-office systems
- Search terms:
  - `low voltage contractor`
  - `security system installer commercial`
  - `access control installer`
  - `AV integrator`
  - `structured cabling contractor`
  - `fire alarm installer`
- Safe source ideas:
  - Google Places / Outscraper
  - The Blue Book Network and trade directories for manual list building
  - local builder and subcontractor associations
  - manufacturer dealer locators where terms allow manual research
- Outreach angle: tie install milestones, site approvals, completion photos, and payout readiness to one timeline
- Buyer pain: field completion proof sits on phones, final punch items move by text, billing waits on scattered approvals
- Plain-English positioning: show what part of the install is done, what was approved, and what can be billed now

### 3. Pool, spa, deck, fence, and outdoor-living contractors

## Suggested Outreach Framing Rule

Start every first-touch message with the operational problem, not the technology:

- what gets stuck
- what proof is hard to find
- what payout or approval status becomes unclear
- what shared record would remove friction

Only introduce the smart-contract proof layer as a supporting detail after the workflow pain is already clear.
